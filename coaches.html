Head Coach Brian Dunn

Hall of Fame Coach Brian Dunn begins his twelfth season as the USJ softball coach in 2017. His 2016 squad captured the Region and Sectional for the sixth time in the last seven years on their way to a third place state finish. Guiding the Lady Bruins, Coach Dunn owns a career record of 284-78. Other highlights of his USJ tenure include capturing the 2010 and 2012 TSSAA State Championship titles and TSSAA State Runner-Up finshes in 2011 and 2013.

Coach Dunn has a twenty-three year, thirty-one season varsity caoching record in college and high school of 1083-279 including sixteen seasons at Union University. His Union Softball and Volleyball teams won fifteen Conference, six Region, and four National Championships. His teams qualified for seven NAIA National Tournaments. He began his coaching career with three season of softball at Riverside High and led the 1996 team to the school's first appearance in the TSSAA State Tournament. Brian was inducted into the Tennessee Softball Coaches Association Hall of Fame in 2014 and the USJ Softball Hall of Fame in 2016.

He has coached twenty seven collegiate All-Americans, twenty four high school All-State athletes and has been honored as Coach of the Year thirty two times in his career by various organizations.

Brian is married to the former Emily Garrett and they have two children: Ashlee, a junior at USJ and Addison, a freshman studying nursing at Union University on a soccer scholarship.

Assistant Coach Sherry Fendley

Sherry Fendley is married to Tom Fendley. She will begin her first season as an assistant with the USJ upper school softball program, but is no stranger to USJ and working with Coach Dunn. Sherry's son Peyton, a 2013 USJ graduate, played on the 2012 State Championship baseball team with her daughter Sydney, a 2016 USJ graduate, played on the 2013 Runner-up softball team.

Sherry has assisted on several USJ middle school teams and meaned with Coach Dunn on Chris Pickens in 2009 in leading the Madison Central Dixie Angels to a Dixie World Series Championship and a perfect 28-0 summer.
